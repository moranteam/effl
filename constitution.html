Preamble

We the Owners of the Estate, in order to form a more perfect league, establish justice in matters of waivers, ensure domestic tranquility in the group chat, provide for the common defense of the snake draft, and secure the blessings of victory to ourselves and our keepers, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Estate Fantasy Football League, founded in the year 2015 at The Estate, Tempe, Arizona, where its founders lived, argued, and first set lineups together.

Article IThe League

Section 1. The league shall be known as the Estate Fantasy Football League ("EFFL"), operating on the ESPN platform as the ESTATE Fantasy League.

Section 2. The league shall consist of 8 franchises, organized in a single division, the East.

Section 3. Competition shall be head to head, decided on points, with one matchup per week beginning NFL Week 1.

Article IIGovernance

Section 1. The Commissioner. Chen serves as Commissioner and administers the platform, the schedule, and the peace.

Section 2. The Office of the Annotator. Charlie serves as League Annotator and Keeper of the Record. Minutes, standings, prophecies, and Tally Statute compliance are matters of permanent record.

Section 3. Passage of Law. A new league law passes with 7 of 8 votes, per Amendment 2025-IV. A dispute concerning separate thresholds for ownership changes (asserted 8 of 8) and rule changes (asserted 6 of 8) is pending verification; until resolved, the 7 of 8 standard governs all questions.

Section 4. Trade Review. Trades are subject to a 1 day review period. A trade is vetoed upon 6 votes.

Article IIIMembership And Dues

Section 1. Dues for the 2025 season were $250 per owner. Dues for the 2026 season and beyond are subject to final approval after discussions with ownership.

Section 2. The league stands at 8 owners. Expansion to 10 was rejected under Motion 2025-VII. The prospective admission of two new members remains under consideration and would require passage under Section 3 of Article II.

Section 3. No incoming owner shall be required to receive a tattoo as a condition of entry, per Motion 2025-V. Incoming owners are nonetheless subject to the Tally Statute (Article X) from their first season, and should read it carefully before signing anything.

Article IVThe Draft

Section 1. The draft shall be conducted in the snake format, reaffirmed under Motion 2025-II.

Section 2. Each pick is allotted 60 seconds. The draft order is set manually by the league manager.

Section 3. Draft pick trading is disabled on the platform of record. Where the Keeper Codex (Article IX, Section 9) contemplates the exchange of picks in connection with a traded keeper, such exchanges shall be administered manually by the Commissioner.

Article VIIWaivers And Free Agency

Section 1. Player acquisition is governed by a Free Agent Budget of $100 per franchise per season, with a minimum bid of $1 and no season acquisition limit.

Section 2. The waiver period is 1 day. Free Agent Budget ties are broken by inverse order of the standings, resetting each week.

Section 3. Transactions lock for eliminated teams during the playoffs, and for teams not eligible for 3rd place, per the settings of record.

Article VIIITrades

Section 1. There is no limit on the number of trades.

Section 2. The trade deadline for the 2026 season is November 25, 2026 at 9:00 AM Pacific.

Section 3. Trades are subject to a 1 day review period and may be vetoed by 6 votes of the Assembly.

Article IXThe Keeper Codex

Ratified under Motion 2025-VIII, 7 to 1, the first law passed under the modern voting threshold, and promulgated to the Assembly by Commissioner Chen.

Section 1. Eligibility. Only running backs, wide receivers, and tight ends may be kept. Quarterbacks, kickers, and defenses re-enter the pool each year like everyone else.

Section 2. Draft Round Restriction. No player drafted in the 1st or 2nd round may be kept. Keeper eligibility begins in the 3rd round.

Section 3. One Keeper Per Franchise. Each franchise may keep a maximum of one player per season, per the league settings of record.

Section 4. Roster Continuity. A keeper must remain on the keeping owner's roster for the entire season, unless acquired by trade.

Section 5. Cost of Keeping. An owner who keeps a player forfeits the draft round in which that player was originally selected. Each additional consecutive year of keeping escalates the cost by one round. Example: a player drafted in the 8th round costs an 8th round pick in year one, a 7th in year two, and a 6th in year three.

Section 6. Free Agent Keepers. A player acquired as a free agent may be kept at the cost of a 6th round pick.

Section 7. Term Limit. No player may be kept for more than 3 consecutive years. All dynasties end.

Section 8. Declaration Deadline. Keepers must be declared no later than one week before the draft, and in all cases lock one hour before the draft per platform settings.

Section 9. Tradability. Keepers may be traded. The team trading away a keeper shall receive the associated draft pick, administered manually by the Commissioner per Article IV, Section 3.

Article XThe Tally Statute

Section 1. The owner whose franchise finishes in last place at the conclusion of a season shall receive a permanent tattoo of a tally mark.

Section 2. The tally shall measure no less than one inch. Placement is at the discretion of the finisher, with the buttock recognized as the traditional site.

Section 3. One tally is owed per last place finish. Tallies are cumulative over an owner's career and constitute the league's permanent standings of shame.

Section 4. Compliance is a matter of honor and public record. Proof shall be furnished to the Assembly and noted by the Annotator.

Article XISeason And Playoffs

Section 1. The regular season begins NFL Week 1 with one matchup per week and no tie breakers in regular season matchups.

Section 2. Four teams qualify for the playoffs. Playoff rounds are one week each, including the championship.

Section 3. Playoff seeding ties are broken by total points for. There is no home field advantage and no bracket reseeding. A consolation ladder is in effect.
